PREFACE

The following notes are written at the request of some kind and amiable friends, whom I find difficulty in refusing,from the reasonswhich they urged.I was represented to me that the peculiar opportunities which my professional employement gave me of observing the state of slavery.

INTRODUCTION

THE horrors of the unnatural and debasing system which forms the subject of the present publication, have of late years been so completely exposed and so faithfully depicted, by many able and well-informed writers, that it will be the less necessary on the present occasion to enter into any general views on the subject. Mine is the more humble task of detailing such facts in illustra- tion of the effects of the system as have incidentally presented themselves during my residence in the colony of the Cape of Good Hope, and of reviewing the progress of legislative improve- ment within that period, which I have watched with so small degree of interest.

RELIGIOUS INSTRUCTION

ALTHOUGH religious instruction forms no part of the last Con- solidated Order, yet it naturally presents itself to the mind of the Christian as the first subject for consideration. Its nature, progress, and extent must be a subject of interest to many; and it shall be my endeavour to give, if not in all respects a pleasing, yet I trust an impartial, account of these.

PRIZE NEGROES

PUNISHMENTS

Mr. Miller thus forcibly depicts the further inefficacy of pro- tecting laws for the slave. “Distant, perhaps, fifty miles from the nearest civil authority, how can he enter his complaint? And if he do, and punishment is awarded to the master, it were better for him that he had never been born. Alas! may he not now be sacrificed in a thousand ways that the law can take no notice of?-for those who have seen the system working know full well that this is an offence which is never overlooked. He is now subject to every species of misery. His very companions abandon him—for sympathy in his wretchedness may be visited with the lash, and deserted by all- he pines till death release him."
